# Digital Exclusion and Health Equity in Rural Wales

## Project Overview
This repository analyses whether digital exclusion in rural Wales is associated with higher health-related deprivation, using open Welsh Government, Ofcom, and ONS data at LSOA (small-area) level. Produced as part of a 10-week professional placement with Rural Health Compass (RHC), addressing RQ3 of the team's wider work on whether the NHS Wales "Community by Design" strategy is equitable for rural communities.

## Research Question
Is digital exclusion in rural Wales associated with higher levels of health-related deprivation and poorer population health indicators? Specifically: does broadband infrastructure support digital-first healthcare delivery, comparing rural areas in Powys, Ceredigion, and Pembrokeshire against urban Swansea.

## Methods
- **Data:** WIMD 2025 (Welsh Government), Ofcom Connected Nations broadband coverage, ONS Rural/Urban Classification 2021 — joined at LSOA level (1,917 Wales LSOAs; 288 in the primary 4-area study sample: Powys n=69, Ceredigion n=34, Pembrokeshire n=54, Swansea-urban n=131).
- **Comparator design:** the project uses three different grouping structures for different questions, each documented in its own script — a 2-group pooled rural/urban design, a 3-group design (rural / county-urban market towns / Swansea-urban), and a 4-group design treating each area separately. See `process_report.md` for why each was used.
- **Statistics:** Spearman rank correlation (domain-by-domain, rural vs urban), Kruskal-Wallis tests with epsilon-squared effect sizes across all 8 WIMD domains plus digital exclusion, Wilcoxon pairwise post-hoc comparisons with Benjamini-Hochberg correction.
- **Mapping:** choropleth maps in R (`sf`, `ggplot2`), using national WIMD deciles rather than subset-derived breaks, with corrected colour-scale direction (WIMD rank 1 = most deprived) and labelled area outlines.
